Corpus summary

The documentary presents Wylie Avenue and the Hill District as a dense Black cultural, commercial, social, religious, nightlife, newspaper, sports, and political ecosystem. It describes the neighborhood's rise during the Great Migration, the role of segregation in shaping parallel Black institutions, the importance of Wylie Avenue and Fullerton Street as a crossroads, and the later damage caused by urban renewal, Civic Arena construction, displacement, and the post-1968 decline of the lower Hill.

Research use: strong source for Pittsburgh node selection, people/entity extraction, and social-infrastructure coding. It should not replace address-level archival verification.
