Sanborn Research LLM Corpus Proof of Concept

Archival corpus PDFs/images/transcripts → structured source metadata → deterministic counts/entity extraction → LLM-assisted thematic bins → candidate hubs/corridors → human verification → GIS/story map layer.

This prototype stages the workflow for turning Buffalo, Pittsburgh, and possible intercity corridor evidence into structured research leads. It does not replace archival verification; it organizes and scores evidence so the team can review stronger leads faster.

Updated model logic

StepActionOutput
1. Corpus stagingAdd PDFs, links, archival descriptions, documentary transcript pages, event listings, corridor notes, newspaper clippings, and source metadata.Source inventory with city, topic, date, status, and evidence notes.
2. Deterministic extractionUse Python/text extraction where available; visually structure image-based clippings; count terms/entities; flag unreadable, image-only, or copyrighted full-transcript sources for review-safe metadata handling.Keyword counts, entity tables, event rows, source coverage, and verification flags.
3. Thematic binningUse controlled theme dictionaries plus LLM-assisted summaries.Labor, regulation, protest, segregation, venue, corridor, institution, preservation, displacement, performance, media, social infrastructure, and intercity-network bins.
4. Node/corridor scoringScore by recurrence, source count, evidence strength, named performers/orgs, address readiness, social-infrastructure role, displacement relevance, and map readiness.Tier I hub, Tier II node, supporting venue, local corridor, displacement zone, or intercity corridor lead.
5. Human verificationResearchers verify address, dates, source excerpt, city links, and confidence.GIS-ready node and corridor table.
6. Spatial handoffExport verified nodes/routes/zones to GeoJSON/QGIS/Leaflet/story map layers.Mapped cultural hubs, corridors, displacement zones, and evidence-backed spatial/network analysis.
